# Kafka from Zero to Confident

*A hands-on tutorial that takes you from "what is Kafka?" to confidently understanding distributed event streaming — in under an hour of reading.*

## Who This Is For

You're a developer, data engineer, or curious technologist who keeps hearing about Kafka but hasn't had time to sit down and learn it properly. No prior Kafka experience needed.

## Prerequisites

- Basic understanding of client-server architecture
- Familiarity with any programming language (CLI examples used)
- Docker installed for the hands-on sections

## Roadmap

| # | Lesson | What You'll Learn |
|---|--------|-------------------|
| 1 | What Is Kafka & Why It Exists | The problem Kafka solves and where it fits |
| 2 | Thinking in Events | Shift from request/response to event-driven thinking |
| 3 | Topics & Messages | How data is organized and what a message looks like |
| 4 | Partitions | How topics split for parallelism and ordering |
| 5 | Brokers & Clusters | Servers, replication, and fault tolerance |
| 6 | Producers | How data gets into Kafka |
| 7 | Consumers | Reading data with offsets and polling |
| 8 | Consumer Groups | Scaling reads with parallel consumers |
| 9 | Your First Cluster | Spin up Kafka locally with Docker |
| 10 | Produce & Consume Messages | Hands-on: send and read your first messages |
| 11 | Real-World Use Cases | Patterns you'll see in production |
| 12 | What's Next | Key takeaways and further learning |

## Estimated Time

~45 minutes total (12 lessons, ~3-4 minutes each)
