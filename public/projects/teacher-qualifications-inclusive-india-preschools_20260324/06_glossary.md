## Category A: Institutions and Bodies

**ADAPT (formerly Spastics Society of India)**
: Non-governmental organization founded 1972, Mumbai. Runs India's oldest integrated preschool and school for children with cerebral palsy and developmental disabilities. Operates inclusive ECCE programs and trains government ECCE workers in inclusive practice.

**Ability Foundation**
: NGO based in Chennai, Tamil Nadu. Develops and delivers disability inclusion programs for ECCE settings including the 2024 AWW Disability Detection Protocol. Known for community-based early intervention and AWW capacity building.

**DSEL (Department of School Education and Literacy)**
: Division of India's Ministry of Education responsible for school education policy. Released inclusive education guidelines (2023) covering government schools including Bal Vatika/preschool classes.

**IGNOU (Indira Gandhi National Open University)**
: Distance education university offering ECCE programs: CESE (Certificate in Early Childhood Special Education), DPSE (Diploma in Pre-School Education), B.Ed Special Education. Key provider for rural/semi-urban ECCE workforce qualification upgrading.

**MWCD (Ministry of Women and Child Development)**
: Union ministry responsible for ICDS and the Anganwadi system. Governs AWW recruitment, training (via NIPCCD), and honorarium rates. A distinct ministry from MoE — the source of much inter-ministerial coordination failure in ECCE.

**NCERT (National Council of Educational Research and Training)**
: Autonomous body under MoE. Develops national curriculum frameworks including NCF 2005, NCF-FS 2022, and ECCE teaching-learning materials. Published foundational ECCE documents used as training content.

**NCPCR (National Commission for Protection of Child Rights)**
: Statutory body monitoring RTE implementation and child rights. Limited specific ECCE/preschool mandate but oversight of RTE Section 11 compliance.

**NCTE (National Council for Teacher Education)**
: Statutory body under MoE regulating teacher education institutions and setting minimum qualification norms. Governs school-based preschool teacher qualifications; does NOT cover AWWs.

**NIMH (National Institute for the Mentally Handicapped)**
: Hyderabad-based national institute under Ministry of Social Justice & Empowerment. Now called National Institute for Empowerment of Persons with Intellectual Disabilities (NIEPID). Conducts research and training on intellectual disabilities including early childhood.

**NIPCCD (National Institute of Public Cooperation and Child Development)**
: National institute under MWCD. Designs and delivers AWW induction training curriculum (6-month residential program). Has regional centers across India. Key entry point for improving AWW disability competency.

**NITI Aayog**
: Government of India policy think-tank. Published ECCE quality reports including 2017 analysis documenting AWW turnover and compensation inadequacy.

**RCI (Rehabilitation Council of India)**
: Statutory council under Ministry of Social Justice & Empowerment. Governs education and training of special education professionals. Certifies B.Ed (Special Education) and rehabilitation professional programs. Operates separately from NCTE, creating governance fragmentation.

## Category B: Policy and Legal Frameworks

**NCF-FS (National Curriculum Framework for the Foundational Stage) 2022**
: Curriculum framework released October 2022. Covers ages 3–8 (Anganwadi + preschool + Grades 1–2). Mandates play-based, activity-based, inquiry-based pedagogy; UDL principles; mother-tongue medium instruction; and integration of ECCE with early primary education. The operative curriculum framework for ECCE in India as of 2026.

**NEP 2020 (National Education Policy 2020)**
: Comprehensive education reform policy. Chapter 1 mandates universal ECCE by 2030, 2-year ECCE diploma for all ECCE workers, pay parity with primary teachers, and play-based NCF-FS aligned pedagogy. Ambitious but lacks budget allocation and enforcement mechanisms for inclusion.

**RPWD Act 2016 (Rights of Persons with Disabilities Act)**
: Rights-based disability legislation replacing PWD Act 1995. Aligned with UN CRPD. Section 16: inclusive education mandate. Section 31: government must train all teachers in disability inclusion. Covers all education stages including preschool — but enforcement for 3–6 age group has jurisdictional gaps relative to RTE.

**RTE Act 2009 (Right of Children to Free and Compulsory Education Act)**
: Constitutional education right for ages 6–14. Section 11: states should provide ECCE for under-6 (non-binding). Does not create enforceable rights for 3–6 age group. Vidhi Centre recommendation: amend RTE to extend to age 3.

## Category C: Programs and Systems

**ICDS (Integrated Child Development Services)**
: Government of India's flagship child development program, launched 1974. Delivers ECCE, supplementary nutrition, health check-ups, immunization support, and family counseling through the Anganwadi system. World's largest ECCE delivery program by enrollment scale.

**RBSK (Rashtriya Bal Swasthya Karyakram)**
: School health screening program launched 2013. Screens children aged 0–18 including Anganwadi children for 4Ds: Defects at Birth, Diseases, Deficiencies, Developmental Delays and Disabilities. Has screened 170 million children. Referral to District Early Intervention Centers (DEICs) — but follow-up rates are poor.

**TDSC (Trivandrum Developmental Screening Chart)**
: Validated Indian developmental screening tool for 0–6 age group. Most widely used screening tool in AWC/primary health center setting. Limited sensitivity for subtle developmental delays; designed for community-level use by non-specialists.

## Category D: Pedagogical and Assessment Concepts

**CLASS (Classroom Assessment Scoring System)**
: Observational tool developed by Pianta et al. (University of Virginia) for assessing quality of teacher-child interactions. Measures: Emotional Support, Classroom Organization, Instructional Support. Used in ECCE quality research; not yet widely validated or used in India.

**ECERS-R (Early Childhood Environment Rating Scale — Revised)**
: Observational quality rating tool for preschool environments. 7 subscales including Space/Furnishings, Language/Reasoning, Activities, Interactions, Programme Structure, Parents/Staff, and Provisions for Special Needs. Used in research studies assessing Indian AWC/preschool quality.

**IEP (Individualized Education Program)**
: Document mandated by RPWD 2016 for students with disabilities. Specifies learning goals, support services, accommodations, and assessment modifications tailored to individual child. In ECCE context: adapted as Individualized Family Service Plan (IFSP) for 0–3 or IEP for 3–6.

**UDL (Universal Design for Learning)**
: Framework developed by CAST (US) for designing learning environments that are accessible to all learners from the outset, without requiring retrofitting. Three principles: Multiple Means of Representation (how information is presented), Action and Expression (how learners demonstrate knowledge), Engagement (why learners are motivated). Mandated in NCF-FS 2022 for all ECCE settings.

## Category E: Workforce Terms

**AWH (Anganwadi Helper)**
: Support worker at each AWC assisting AWW with nutrition distribution, cleaning, and general maintenance. Lower qualification than AWW (8th pass typically). Paid even lower honorarium. Also referred to as "Mini AWW" in some states.

**AWW (Anganwadi Worker)**
: Community-based ECCE and child development worker deployed at each Anganwadi Center. Minimum 10th-pass qualification; 6-month NIPCCD training post-appointment. Receives honorarium (not salary): Rs 4,500–10,000/month. Performs 8 ICDS functions including ECCE delivery.

**D.El.Ed (Diploma in Elementary Education)**
: 2-year pre-service teacher education diploma for teaching Grades 1–8. Also offered with pre-primary/ECCE specialization track for school-based preschool teachers. Governed by NCTE. Delivery through DIETs (District Institutes of Education and Training).

**DIET (District Institute of Education and Training)**
: District-level teacher education institution under state education departments. Delivers D.El.Ed and in-service teacher training. Also responsible for ECCE teacher education programs at district level.

**NTT (Nursery Teacher Training)**
: 1-year post-12th certificate for preschool/nursery teachers. Less rigorous than D.El.Ed. Widely accepted in private preschool sector. No disability inclusion content mandated in NTT curriculum nationally.

**Resource Teacher / Special Educator**
: Teacher with B.Ed Special Education or equivalent RCI-certified qualification. Provides individualized support to students with disabilities in inclusive settings. Critical support role largely absent from India's ECCE sector (35,000 for 8 million children with disabilities aged 0–6).

## Category F: Disability and Inclusion Concepts

**Deficit Framing of Disability**
: Perspective that views disability as a personal deficiency or medical problem requiring "fixing." Contrasted with the social model. Research shows deficit framing in teachers predicts poor inclusive practice; training that challenges this framing produces lasting positive attitude change.

**Developmental Screening**
: Brief, standardized procedure to identify children who may have developmental delays requiring further evaluation. Distinguished from diagnostic assessment (which identifies specific conditions). TDSC is India's main community-level screening tool.

**Early Identification**
: Detection of developmental delays or disabilities in children aged 0–6 before formal schooling. Critical because neuroplasticity is highest in 0–3 years; early intervention in this window produces greatest developmental gains.

**Inclusive Education**
: Education model where children with and without disabilities learn together in the same setting, with appropriate support and accommodations. Mandated by RPWD 2016, CRPD, and NEP 2020 for all stages including ECCE.

**Social Model of Disability**
: Framework that locates disability in society's failure to accommodate human diversity, rather than in the individual. An AWC lacks ramps → the environment disables a child using a wheelchair, not the disability itself. Contrast: medical model (disability is the person's "problem").
