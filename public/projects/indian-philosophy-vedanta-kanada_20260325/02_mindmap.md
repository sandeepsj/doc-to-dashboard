# Indian Philosophy — Mind Map

Indian philosophy is an intricate web of ideas that developed over 3,500 years. This visual map helps you see the whole forest before examining individual trees. The first diagram shows how the major traditions, schools, concepts, and figures connect. The second diagram shows the *causal chain* — how ignorance leads to suffering, and how wisdom leads to liberation.

## Explanation of Major Branches

### Six Darshanas — The Orthodox Schools

The six schools are all **Astika** (accepting Vedic authority). They developed from roughly 600 BCE to 500 CE, often as responses to each other. **Nyaya** and **Vaisheshika** pair naturally (logic + ontology). **Samkhya** and **Yoga** pair naturally (cosmology + practice). **Mimamsa** and **Vedanta** pair naturally (earlier Vedas + later Vedas). The six are sometimes called three pairs for this reason.

### Vedas and Upanishads — The Textual Bedrock

The four Vedas are the authoritative foundation that all Astika schools accept. The Upanishads (the philosophical conclusions of the Vedas) are the *primary data source* for Vedanta — every Vedanta philosopher wrote a commentary on the principal Upanishads to establish their school's interpretation. The debate between Shankara, Ramanuja, and Madhva is essentially: "What do these Upanishadic sentences *really mean*?"

### Key Concepts — The Core Language

Brahman, Atman, Maya, Karma, Samsara, and Moksha are the six words you need to understand to read any Indian philosophical text. They are to Indian philosophy what "pointer," "memory," "process," "stack," and "heap" are to systems programming — the foundational vocabulary through which everything else is described.

### Vedanta Sub-schools — The Great Debate

All three sub-schools use the same texts (Upanishads, Bhagavad Gita, Brahma Sutras — together called the **Prasthanatrayi**, or "triple canon"). All three claim to be the correct interpretation. The difference is the relationship between Brahman and Atman:
- **Advaita:** Identical (Shankara, Kerala)
- **Vishishtadvaita:** Distinct-but-inseparable, like soul-and-body (Ramanuja, Tamil Nadu)
- **Dvaita:** Eternally distinct (Madhva, Karnataka)

Fascinatingly, all three great teachers of Vedanta came from South India — and their debates shaped the theological and devotional culture of the entire subcontinent.

### The Causal Chain — How the Loop Works

The flowchart above traces the complete mechanics of existence according to Indian philosophy. Starting from **Avidya** (root ignorance), the system bootstraps the experience of a separate self, which generates karma, which drives rebirth, which perpetuates suffering. The four yogas are four different "patches" applied at different points in the chain. All four lead to **Viveka** (discrimination between real and unreal) and **Vairagya** (dispassion toward the unreal), which deepen into **Jnana** (direct knowledge), which produces **Moksha**. And Moksha reveals that **Brahman** was the ground all along — which means Avidya was never ultimately real.
